Smart Home Energy Prediction is a complete machine learning pipeline that synthesizes smart home sensor data, preprocesses it into feature-rich datasets, and trains multiple regression models to accurately forecast household energy consumption. The pipeline spans six phases — from sensor emulation and MQTT streaming to model training, evaluation, and feature analysis.

## How it works

The pipeline runs six sequential phases, each building on the last:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate sensor data">
    Emulate two years of smart home readings — temperature, humidity, occupancy, and appliance power — at 5-minute intervals across five rooms.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stream via MQTT (optional)">
    Publish sensor readings to a Mosquitto broker and subscribe to collect them in real time, simulating a live IoT deployment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preprocess and engineer features">
    Load raw CSVs, detect format, resample to a consistent frequency, and engineer time-based and aggregate features for ML.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Train and tune models">
    Train Linear Regression, Random Forest, and MLP Regressor models with GridSearchCV hyperparameter tuning and 5-fold cross-validation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Evaluate and compare">
    Measure R², RMSE, and MAE against defined targets. Run paired t-tests to determine statistically significant performance differences.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Analyse feature importance">
    Use permutation importance and Oh's decomposition to identify which sensor features drive energy predictions.
  </Step>
</Steps>
